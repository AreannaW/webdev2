Interview with Kimberly

Overview

People can connect with nature, find comfort, and build growing sanctuaries using the age-old hobby of gardening. A 55-year-old Chicagoan from the south side of the city shares her motivational journey. Kimberly's love of gardening has completely changed her life and inspired her to devote her life to nature preservation as a public school counselor, Illinois Master Gardener, and Master Naturalist. The purpose of this article is to examine how Kimberly's childhood, favorite plant, suggestions for aspiring gardeners, noteworthy experiences, and future goals have influenced her transformational relationship with gardening and nature. Furthermore, it shows the significant influence gardening has had on her life and the message she wants to share with others who have similar interests.

Kimberly's Gardening Journey:

Born and raised in Chicago, Kimberly was exposed to gardening at an early age through the influence of her parents and grandparents. Witnessing their love for gardening inspired her and planted a seed within her own heart. However, it was later in life, as she sought to reevaluate and manage stress, that she wholeheartedly embraced gardening as a therapeutic outlet.

Favorite Plant and Cultural Significance:

For Kimberly, native plants hold a special place in her heart, particularly the enchanting butterfly weed milkweed. As a Latina woman, Kimberly deeply appreciates the cultural and ancestral significance of the monarch butterfly's migration from her region to Mexico. This connection to her heritage and the natural world intertwines, providing her with a sense of belonging and purpose.

Her Advice for Creating Butterfly Sanctuaries:

The goal and motive of developing a butterfly sanctuary or a garden that is friendly to creatures, in Kimberly's opinion, are crucial. The first step is to understand why one wishes to go on this adventure. The "why" acts as guidance, whether it be a pastime or a goal to design a pollinator-friendly area. Additionally, Kimberly highlights the importance of community involvement, emphasizing how involving more people fosters shared responsibility and the chance to instruct and motivate others.

Memorable Experiences and Encounters:

Kimberly's garden has become a hub of curiosity and connection. Even without owning a house, she has managed to captivate her community with her gardening endeavors. People from diverse backgrounds inquire about her passion, leading to meaningful conversations and a deeper appreciation for the wonders of nature. Kimberly takes pride in showcasing the miraculous occurrences that unfold within her sanctuary, leaving visitors in awe of the beauty and interconnectedness of the natural world.

Future Plans and Goals:

Kimberly aspires to broaden her impact and share her experiences beyond her immediate surroundings. By spreading awareness and fostering connections between humans and the non-human world, she aims to inspire others to cherish and care for nature. Kimberly firmly believes that if we nurture and protect nature, it, in turn, will take care of us.

Impact of Gardening and Conservation:

Kimberly's life has been incredibly joyful and deeply fulfilled as a result of gardening. Her life has been enriched by being able to see plants as they grow, get to know them, and experience the transformational force of nature. She encourages people to set aside time to spend in nature, highlighting the fact that it cannot be avoided, and she encourages them to spread their love of the natural world to others. Sharing personal experiences with nature, according to Kimberly, can help people appreciate and comprehend how intertwined all living things are.
